759 Andrew Grove (1936-2016) was a Hungarian-American businessman and engineer who co-founded the semiconductor company Intel Corporation in 1968.  Grove served as CEO of Intel from 1987 to 1998 and as Chairman of the Board from 1997 to 2005. Grove was born in Hungary and survived the Holocaust before emigrating to the United States in 1957.  He earned a Bachelor of Chemical Engineering degree from the City College of New York and a Ph. D.  in chemical engineering from the University of California  Berkeley.
