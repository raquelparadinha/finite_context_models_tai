915 E-government  or electronic government  refers to the use of digital technologies  such as the internet  mobile devices  and other digital communication channels  to improve the delivery of government services to citizens and businesses.  It involves the use of technology to provide public services  including online forms  databases  and other digital resources. E-government can enhance government transparency  efficiency  and responsiveness  as well as increase citizen participation in the policymaking process.  It also offers citizens and businesses more convenient and accessible ways to interact with government services  such as paying taxes  accessing public records  and submitting applications for permits and licenses. Examples of e-government services include online voting  electronic tax filing  online health records  digital identity management  and online public service delivery.
